# 📝 Lesson Content Guide — For Marie

Hey Marie! This guide explains how to write lessons for the Youth Educated app. Each lesson teaches Kenyan youth (ages 10–22) a life skill in a short, engaging format.

---

## How a Lesson Works

Each lesson has **4 sections** in this exact order:

| # | Section | What It Is | Length |
|---|---------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **Text** | The main lesson content. Conversational, relatable, with Kenyan examples. | 2–3 paragraphs |
| 2 | **Pullquote** | An inspiring or memorable quote that captures the lesson's message. | 1 sentence |
| 3 | **Insight Prompt** | A reflective question that makes the student think about their own life. | 1 question |
| 4 | **Quiz** | A multiple-choice question (3 options, 1 correct). | 1 question + 3 options |

### Writing Tips
- **Tone**: Like a cool older sibling — warm, encouraging, not preachy
- **Context**: Use Kenyan references (M-Pesa, matatus, counties, Sheng, CBC system, local heroes like Kipchoge, Wangari Maathai)
- **Length**: Keep text sections to 2–3 short paragraphs. Students will read this on their phones
- **Age awareness**: Some modules are for all ages (10+), some are 13+ or 16+ — keep language appropriate
- **Duration**: Each lesson should take about **6 minutes** to read and complete

## Example Lesson (Copy This Structure)

Here's a real lesson from the **Confidence Building** module:

```
Lesson Title: "What is Confidence?"
Duration: 6 min

TEXT:
Confidence is not about being perfect — it's about trusting yourself
even when you make mistakes. Think about a matatu conductor who calls
out stops confidently even in heavy traffic. That confidence comes
from doing it over and over, not from knowing everything.

In Kenya, many young people feel pressure to have all the answers.
But real confidence means being okay with not knowing and still
stepping forward. It's the voice inside that says "I can figure this out."

PULLQUOTE:
"Confidence is a muscle. The more you use it, the stronger it gets."

INSIGHT PROMPT:
Think of a time you felt scared but did it anyway. How did you feel after?

QUIZ:
Question: Which of these is a sign of healthy confidence?
A) Never admitting you are wrong
B) Trying new things even if you are scared  ← CORRECT
C) Comparing yourself to others on social media
```
